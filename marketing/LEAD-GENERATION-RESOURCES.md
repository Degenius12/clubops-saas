# Lead Generation Resources for ClubFlow

## State Liquor License Databases (PRIMARY SOURCE)

### Top 10 States by Club Count:

**1. California** (500+ clubs)
- Website: abc.ca.gov
- Path: License Lookup → Search by License Type → "48 - On-Sale General" (filter results)
- Data: Business name, DBA, address, phone, license holder
- Tip: Search for "cabaret" in business name field

**2. Nevada** (250+ clubs, Las Vegas heavy)
- Website: ccb.nv.gov/licensing
- Path: License Search → Adult Entertainment
- Data: Business name, location, owner name, status
- Tip: Most are in Clark County (Vegas)

**3. Texas** (300+ clubs)
- Website: tabc.texas.gov
- Path: License & Permit Search → Late Hours Permit
- Data: Permit holder, trade name, address, phone
- Tip: Cross-reference with "sexually oriented business" permits (citywide)

**4. Florida** (250+ clubs)
- Website: myfloridalicense.com/dbpr
- Path: Search Licenses → Alcoholic Beverages → Special License
- Data: Business name, address, owner, phone
- Tip: Most in Miami-Dade, Broward, Hillsborough counties

**5. Louisiana** (100+ clubs)
- Website: atc.louisiana.gov
- Path: Public Records → License Search
- Data: Very detailed including owner contact
- Tip: New Orleans has highest concentration

**6. Illinois** (100+ clubs, Chicago heavy)
- Website: ilcc.illinois.gov
- Path: Licensing → License Lookup
- Data: Business name, address, licensee
- Tip: Look for "adult entertainment" endorsement

**7. New York** (150+ clubs, NYC heavy)
- Website: sla.ny.gov (State Liquor Authority)
- Path: Online Services → License Search
- Data: Business name, address, principals
- Tip: NYC requires separate cabaret license (harder to find)

**8. Oregon** (50+ clubs, Portland heavy)
- Website: oregon.gov/olcc
- Path: License Search
- Data: Business name, address, licensee
- Tip: Portland is most permissive city

**9. Georgia** (80+ clubs, Atlanta heavy)
- Website: dor.georgia.gov/alcohol-tobacco
- Path: License Verification
- Data: Business name, location, owner
- Tip: DeKalb County (Atlanta) has most clubs

**10. Washington** (60+ clubs, Seattle area)
- Website: lcb.wa.gov
- Path: License Search
- Data: Business name, address, license type
- Tip: King County (Seattle) and Pierce County (Tacoma)

### How to Use Liquor Databases:

1. **Search Strategy**:
   - Filter by "late hours" or "2am" permits
   - Look for business names with keywords: "gentlemen's", "cabaret", "adult"
   - Cross-reference with Google Maps to confirm

2. **Data Export**:
   - Most allow CSV download
   - If not, copy/paste to Google Sheets
   - Use "Data Miner" Chrome extension for scraping

3. **Contact Info**:
   - License holder name = usually owner or LLC registered agent
   - Phone on license = business phone (call, ask for owner/GM)
   - Address = mailing address (sometimes personal, sometimes club)

## LinkedIn Search Strategies

### Boolean Search Formulas:

**Find Club Owners**:
```
(owner OR founder OR "club owner") AND (gentlemen's club OR nightclub OR cabaret) AND (city OR state)
```

**Find General Managers**:
```
("general manager" OR GM) AND (gentlemen's club OR nightclub OR adult entertainment OR cabaret)
```

**Find Multi-Location Operators**:
```
(owner OR operator) AND (clubs OR locations OR venues) AND (entertainment OR hospitality)
```

### LinkedIn Search Tips:
1. **2nd-degree connections**: Higher acceptance rate
2. **Current company**: Search by club name (if known)
3. **Location**: Filter by city
4. **Industry**: Filter by "Entertainment" or "Hospitality"
5. **Recent activity**: Post engagement shows active users

### Free LinkedIn Hacks:

- Save searches (get notifications for new matches)
- Export connections via Settings (CSV of your network)
- Use Google: `site:linkedin.com "general manager" "gentlemen's club" "Las Vegas"`

## Email Finder Tools

### Free Tier Tools:

**1. Hunter.io** (hunter.io)
- Free: 25 searches/month
- Best for: Finding email patterns for domains
- Use: Enter club website, get common email format
- Example: If johndoe@clubname.com exists, try owner@clubname.com

**2. Apollo.io** (apollo.io)
- Free: 50 credits/month
- Best for: B2B contact database
- Use: Search by job title + company name
- Bonus: CRM functionality included

**3. Clearbit Connect** (clearbit.com/connect)
- Free: 100 searches/month (Gmail extension)
- Best for: Quick lookups during email composition
- Use: Type name in Gmail "To" field, suggests emails

**4. Snov.io** (snov.io)
- Free: 50 credits/month
- Best for: Email verification (avoid bounces)
- Use: Verify emails before sending

**5. RocketReach** (rocketreach.co)
- Free: 5 lookups/month
- Best for: Executive contact info
- Use: LinkedIn profile → RocketReach → email/phone

### Free Manual Methods:

**1. Website Contact Form**:
- Fill out club's contact form
- Ask to speak with owner/GM about business opportunity
- Get direct email/phone in response

**2. Call and Ask**:
- Call club during off-hours (2-5pm)
- "Hi, I'm trying to reach the owner/GM about a business opportunity. What's the best email?"
- 70% will give it to you

**3. LinkedIn Message**:
- Connect with GM on LinkedIn
- Once connected, send message asking for email
- Works if you've engaged with their content first

**4. Email Pattern Guessing**:
- Common formats: firstname@clubname.com, owner@clubname.com, info@clubname.com
- Test with mail-tester.com before bulk sending
- Verify with Snov.io (free tier)

- [ ] Recent reviews exist (active business)

- [ ] Liquor license is current (not expired/suspended)

## Legal & Compliance Notes

**What's Legal**:
- ✅ Public business records (liquor licenses)
- ✅ Publicly listed contact info (Google, Yelp)
- ✅ Business emails (owner@clubname.com)
- ✅ LinkedIn profiles (public information)

**What to Avoid**:
- ❌ Personal emails from private databases
- ❌ Scraped personal phone numbers
- ❌ Breaking website terms of service
- ❌ Ignoring unsubscribe requests

**Best Practice**: Only use business contact information for business purposes. Respect opt-outs immediately.
